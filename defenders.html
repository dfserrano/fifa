Constellation of defenders

Data Sheet

Description

The visualization shows the defensive players of each of the countries that will be playing the FIFA World Cup Russia 2018.

The players are organized as if they form a constellation of stars, by using dimensionality reduction called Princial Component Analysis (PCA). With these technique I could represent more than 30 skills of the players (for example, acceleration, ball control, dribbling, speed, etc.) into a 2-D plane. The main goal of PCA is to reduce the dimensionality, while retaining the variation present in the dataset, up to the maximum extent.

Hence, having Sergio Ramos as a reference (top-center), we would say that players close to him are also good defenders, while defenders who are far from them may not be as good. Then, we can see that Germany has probably the best defenders.

Instructions

Users can zoom in/out by using the mouse wheel, and move the constellation by dragging and dropping the background.

When users hover one of the flags, the player information is shown, and when the mouse moves out of the flag, the player information card disappears.
Data Sources

The data is provided by Aman Shrivastava, who extracted the data from sofifa.com (FIFA18) and is made available in Kaggle.

Methodology

From the dataset, I selected the top-3 players, based on the overall score, for each country participating in the World Cup.

Only the players that contain any of the following positions were considered: CB (center back), LCB (left center back), RCB (right center back.

Then, all the attributes that are not part of the profile information, such as strength and stamina, are standardized and fed into the PCA algorithm.

Subsequently, the positions are adjusted in order to have top players at the top-center area of the screen, which is more intuitive for final users.

More details can be deduced from the source code.
